Scattering of photon: تناثر/انتشار-تناثر الفوتونات

When a moving particle encounters another particle on its path, they may collide and scatter, causing the path of the moving particle to be deflected from its original path. This phenomenon is known as photon scattering.

Notes: A good example is the change in the original path of sunlight after colliding with particles present in clouds.

Examples
1. The scattering of photons from charged particles is called Compton scattering.
2. Compton scattering is the result of a high-energy photon colliding with a target, which releases loosely bound electrons from the outer shell of the atom or molecule.
3. Photons reflected from a solar cell due to Compton Scattering are at a lower energy.
