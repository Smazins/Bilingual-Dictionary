Photoelectric Effect التأثير الكهروضوئي

The Photoelectric effect is the phenomenon of emission of electrons from a metal surface when it is illuminated by electromagnetic radiation (photon) with suitable energy.

Examples
1. There are many factors that determine photoelectric effect, including the light's strength, frequency, and intensity.
2. Einstein was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his discovery of the law of the photoelectric effect
3. The photoelectric effect occurs at any frequency, provided intensity of incident light is sufficiently high.

التأثير الكهروضوئي هو ظاهرة انبعاث الإلكترونات من سطح معدني عندما يضيء بالإشعاع الكهرومغناطيسي (الفوتون) بطاقة مناسبة.
