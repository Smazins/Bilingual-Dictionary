Collision: الاصطدام

An occurrence in which two or more particles collide with each other, resulting in a change in their motion or position or properties.

Notes: The interactions between the subatomic particles in nuclear reactors is an example of collision. There are two types of collisions: Elastic collision where both kinetic energy and momentum are conserved and Inelastic collision in which kinetic energy is not conserved as some of it is transferred to other forms of energy such as thermal energy and internal energy.

Examples
1. The left side of this equation is the momentum of the system right before the collision.
2. Inelastic collision results in an energy transfer between the photon and the electron.
3. Electrons traveling slowly change direction after elastic collisions.
