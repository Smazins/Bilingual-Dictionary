Bohr Radius: نصف قطر بور

A fundamental constant that defines the most probable distance between the electron and the nucleus in a hydrogen atom. It is a characteristic length scale used in atomic physics.

Notes: The Bohr radius, a_0=5.29 10-11 m.

Examples
1. Bohr radius is the lowest orbit in the Bohr model of the hydrogen atom.
2. Bohr radius is only relevant to atoms and ions with a single electron, such as hydrogen.
3. The Bohr radius for the hydrogen atom is regarded as an essential physical constant.
