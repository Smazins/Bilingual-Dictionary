The particles that make up atoms, the building blocks of the universe. Subatomic particles have the smallest units of matter and energy, and they are classified as elementary particles and composite particles. Elementary particles include electrons, quarks, leptons, photons, gluon, W and Z bosons, and Higgs bosons. Protons, Neutrons, and Mesons are called composite particles.

Notes: Atoms are composed of three subatomic particles: protons, neutrons and electrons. More than 200 subatomic particles have been detected so far.

Examples
1. Electrons are negatively charged subatomic particles.
2. Subatomic particles also exhibit wave-particle duality similar to photons.
3. Electrons are the lightest of all three subatomic particles.
