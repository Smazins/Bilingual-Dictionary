A subatomic particle that carries a negative electric charge, found in atomic orbitals which are the regions of space located around the nucleus.

Notes: It is one of the fundamental constituents of atoms and plays a crucial role in electrical conductivity and chemical reactions. It was discovered by J. J. Thomson in 1897.

Examples
1. The charge of an electron depends on the cloud of photons, electrons and positrons around that electron
2. Compton scattering results in the loss of an electron.
3. While an electron is revolving in orbit, it neither gains nor loses energy.
