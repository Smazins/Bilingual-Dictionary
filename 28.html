Pauli’s Exclusion Principle: مبدأ استبعاد باولي

This principle explains the arrangement of electrons in atoms. It states that no two electrons can have the same quantum state simultaneously in an orbital.

Notes: Pauli’s Exclusion Principle plays a fundamental role in determining the stability of matter or atoms. In quantum physics, the Pauli’s Exclusion Principle is an important principle, formulated by the Austrian physicist Wolfgang Pauli in the year 1925.

Examples
1. There are subatomic particles without spin, but they do not obey the exclusion principle.
2. In early 1925, Pauli proposed his famous exclusion principle for the first time.
3. Electron pairs occupy the same molecular orbital without violating the Pauli exclusion principle.
