Atomic Orbital: المدار الذري - موقع الإلكترون في الذرة

In quantum physics and atomic theory, atomic orbital is a mathematical term that explains the position and wavelike behaviour of electrons in atoms. Each such orbital has two electrons.

Notes: The first model of an atom was proposed by J. J. Thomson in 1897.

Examples
1. An orbital is the ‘cloud’ of possible locations in which an electron might be found.
2. The shapes of atomic orbitals are a direct consequence of the wave nature of electrons.
3. Atomic orbitals can be uniquely defined by a set of integers known as quantum numbers.

في فيزياء الكم والنظرية الذرية ، المدار الذري هو مصطلح رياضي يشرح الموقع والسلوك الموجي للإلكترونات في الذرات. كل مدار ذري له إلكترونان.
