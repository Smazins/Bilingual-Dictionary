Orbit of an electron: مدار الإلكترون - مسار الإلكترون

The closed path of an electron along which it revolves around the nucleus in an atom (like the Earth revolving around the Sun).

Illustrative Diagram:

Examples
1. The orbit of an electron is so tiny that an electron revolves around the atomic nucleus at an incredible 4 million billion times every second..
2. The number of electrons orbiting a nucleus can be only an integer.
3. The electron in Bohr's circular orbits may be defined as a standing wave.

مسار مغلق للإلكترون يدورفيه حول نواة الذرة (مثل المسار الذي تدور فيه الأرض حول الشمس)
