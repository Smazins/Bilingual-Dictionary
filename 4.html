Bohr’s Model of Atom - Bohr's Structure of Atom – تركيب بور للذر نموذج بور للذرة

According to the Bohr model of the atom, it consists of a central, positively charged nucleus (made up of protons and neutrons), which is surrounded by electrons in their circular orbits.

Notes: Proposed by Niles Bohr in 1913, it is the third improved model of the atom. It avoids the drawbacks of the previous two models by applying the postulates of quantum theory published by Max Plank in 1909.

Examples
1. The Bohr model of the hydrogen atom is one of the most valuable contributions to science.
2. The most accurate atomic model was given by Neil Bohr.
3. The Bohr model was the first to use quantum theory, by postulating that the electrons were limited to specific orbits around the nucleus.

ملاحظة: نموذج بور للذرة اقترحه العالم نيلز بور في عام 1913 ، وهو النموذج المحسن الثالث للذرة.حيث إنه يتجنب عيوب النموذجين السابقين من خلال تطبيق افتراضات نظرية الكم التي نشرها ماكس بلانك في عام 1909.
