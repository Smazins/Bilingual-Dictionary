Planck’s Constant: ثابت بلانك

A fundamental physical constant (denoted as "h") that relates the energy of a photon to its frequency. It plays a crucial role in quantum mechanics and the understanding of the behaviour of particles on atomic and subatomic scales.

Notes: The Planck constant h = 6.626 10-34 J/s.

Examples
1. Planck’s constant is symbolized as h.
2. Planck's constant defines the amount of energy a photon (a particle of light) can carry.
3. Planck’s constant, or h, links a wave or particle’s frequency with its total energy.
